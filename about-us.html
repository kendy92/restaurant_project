About

The idea was quite simple here - setting up an authentic North Indian restaurant that catches both the essence and flavours of Punjab. Why Punjab? The simple answer would be the extremely rich culture. While Toronto has more Indian restaurants than one can fathom, we knew our niche had to be bringing in our combination of homemade spices, recipes from the great northern plains and cooking techniques that help create absolute culinary marvels. After a brief trial period with a fellow food truck, we immediately opened our first outlet in Downtown, which was an instant hit. People appreciated our culinary fusions, innovative representation and overall vibe of Saffron, Downtown. We have to say that no time was wasted in between opening the other 2 outlets - one in Markham and the other one in Etobicoke, as people were travelling quite a distance to dine at Saffron. We are extremely thrilled to have the constant support of all the foodies across Toronto and the rest of Ontario as without them this dream would be just another dream.

I always have been passionate about food. Even when I was in school, I did a number of experiments with my lunches (mostly Mac-n-cheese), and after completing my studies in culinary sciences, I worked for a number of restaurants over 7 years. I am thankful to Bill, my old boss, who persuaded me into starting my own restaurant. The idea behind starting an authentic Indian restaurant was a bit daunting at the beginning, but I was confident in my cooking skills and knew that people would love our food. I couldn't thank my abled team members enough for their continuous innovation in typical North-Indian dishes, their persistence and readiness for working at Saffron.

Henrie Johnson, Owner and Head Chef
